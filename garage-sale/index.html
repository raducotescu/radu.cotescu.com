Monitors - Dell P2715Q - CHF 230 each, CHF 420 together

I have two Dell P2715Q 4K monitors for sale. They're in perfect functioning order and come with all of their original cables and transport boxes. They are the A05 hardware revision, so they do support HDMI 2.0 and it's enabled out of the box.

Dell doesn't list the product any more, but B&H still sells them in the US and has a good specs page.

They have a great IPS panel, a very crisp image, extremely good colour reproduction (they are factory calibrated to 99% sRGB) and overall have a good set of ports, albeit not the most modern ones any more. The stands with which they come allow tilt, pivot, swivel and height adjustments.

Thunderbolt Dock - Caldigit TS3 Plus - CHF 100

You definitely heard about it if you're a Mac user. Once Apple decided to go for the one cable approach, well, we just moved all the cables somewhere else. The dock is the place and it's a pretty good one. There's one caveat with this one: its Ethernet port doesn't seem to work at 1Gbps any more, but rather 100Mbps. As long as that's something that doesn't bother you, then it's the perfect complement to the above monitors if you want to use one cable to rule them all.

The specs are here. The dock comes with its original packaging and cables, but you'll get a Swiss T13 power cord instead of the Schuko one, although if you prefer the latter it can be arranged.

If you want the dock to provide 87W for charging your laptop, then you'll need to update its firmware.

WASD Code v2 87-Key Mechanical Keyboard- CHF 50

It's the keyboard that started the mechanical keyboards craze for me. The result of a collaboration between Weyman Kwong of WASD Keyboards and Jeff “Coding Horror” Atwood, the v2 was quite innovative for its time, a row of DIP switches on the back allowing for a lot of configurations, like:

  • QWERTY, Dvorak or Colemak layouts
  • Mac or PC mode
  • various productivity enhancements

The keyboard comes with Cherry MX Clear switches (tactile, slightly higher actuation force than Cherry MX Brown), all its accessories and original box plus some stabiliser lube. The photos show slight traces of use, since the keycaps are ABS.

For a full list of specs, with the notable difference that v2 came with a USB-A to micro-USB cable (keyboard side), head over to Code Keyboards.

Keychron K2 v1 Mechanical Keyboard - CHF 30

Remember when the pandemics hit and everything, including offices, was closed? Well, I had a Keychron K2 RGB with Gateron Brown switches locked in the office. The keyboard was so cool that I genuinely missed it. So what does a sane person do?! Orders another! But this time I ordered the white backlight version, with PBT keycaps. You won't find it any more, so I guess it's a rarity by now.

A quick run through the specs:

  • Gateron Brown switches - tactile bump, lower actuation force than the Cherry MX Clear from above
  • Bluetooth 4 with support for 3 wireless devices
  • can also run wired - there's an included USB-A to USB-C (device side) cable
  • Mac or PC layout (see the additional keycaps) - there's a side switch for the layout
  • White backlight LEDs with 16 lighting effects
  • PBT keycaps
  • 4000 mAh battery - very good battery life if you don't use the backlight
